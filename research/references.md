# Reference Sites

Curated references for bestCXreviews, grouped by what each one teaches. Bias is toward independent, opinionated, methodology-driven publications, not affiliate listicle sites.

## Editorial voice and business model

- **Stratechery** (stratechery.com). Opinionated, framework-driven analysis. Proof the solo, subscription model works. Study how every piece makes an argument.
- **Benedict Evans** (ben-evans.com). Restrained, essay-like strategic analysis. Free weekly. Good model for spotting second-order effects.
- **The Pragmatic Engineer** (newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com). Deep, research-backed practitioner deep dives. Closest tonal analog to our reviews.
- **Platformer** (platformer.news). Independent accountability journalism. A model for criticizing the companies we cover.
- **The Information** (theinformation.com). Subscription investigative tech journalism. Model for a premium research/report tier on top of articles.

## Review rigor and methodology (the trust anchor)

- **Wirecutter** (nytimes.com/wirecutter). The gold standard. Copy the move of explaining the *how* (how the verdict was reached), buying and testing independently, and the clean "best for most / best budget / best upgrade" format.
- **RTINGS** (rtings.com). Data-driven lab testing with transparent scoring and dense comparison tables. The model for benchmarking AI agents.
- **Consumer Reports** (consumerreports.org). Nonprofit, no ads or affiliate, no manufacturer influence. The purest independence stance for our ethics page.
- **G2 / Capterra / TrustRadius** (g2.com, capterra.com, trustradius.com). The crowd-review aggregators. Treat as what NOT to become. Useful only for category taxonomy and seeing what is listed.

## Competitive landscape (CX and conversational AI outlets)

- **CMSWire** (cmswire.com). CX and digital-experience journalism with named journalists. Heavy AI-in-contact-center coverage.
- **CX Today** (cxtoday.com). CX-industry news, interviews, and podcasts.
- **CustomerThink** (customerthink.com). Large multi-contributor CX thought-leadership community.
- **CX Network** (cxnetwork.com). IQPC membership community. Gated and lead-gen, another model to differentiate from.
- **Everest Group / Gartner / Forrester** (everestgrp.com, gartner.com, forrester.com). Analyst firms behind the PEAK Matrix, Magic Quadrant, and Wave. Position our reviews as the faster, plainer-spoken, free alternative.

## Design references (how it should look)

- **SiteInspire** (siteinspire.com). Selective, editorial, typographic, content-hierarchy-first. Best match for our ink/paper/emerald direction.
- **Godly** (godly.website). Modern, bold startup and SaaS sites. For the product side of the look.
- **Awwwards** (awwwards.com). Great for craft. Caveat: many award sites convert poorly, so pair with conversion sense.
- **Mobbin** (mobbin.com). Real production-app screens. Highest-leverage reference for any interactive UI (e.g. a comparison tool).

## Where to start

Internalize four first: Stratechery for voice, Wirecutter for review methodology, RTINGS for data presentation, SiteInspire for visual language.
