The containment number is lying to you

"70 percent containment" sounds like success. Often it means 70 percent of customers gave up before reaching a human. Here is how to tell the difference.

By The editorsUpdated May 2026

Containment, sometimes called deflection, is the headline metric every AI agent vendor leads with. It measures the share of conversations that did not reach a human agent. The problem is right there in the definition: a conversation can avoid a human because the customer was helped, or because the customer gave up. Containment counts both as a win.

Deflection measures what did not happen to your staff. It says nothing about what happened to your customer.

The two numbers that actually matter

To know whether containment is real, pair it with two metrics the native dashboard tends to bury:

  • Follow-up-contact rate. Of the conversations the AI "resolved," how many customers came back within, say, seven days about the same issue? A high rate means your containment is hiding abandonment and reopened problems.
  • Satisfaction on AI-handled conversations, measured separately. Not blended with human-handled tickets, which masks the difference.

A worked example

ToolContainmentFollow-up rateReal resolution
Tool A72%28%52%
Tool B61%9%56%

Illustrative figures. Tool A wins the metric every vendor quotes and loses the one that matters. The lower follow-up rate makes Tool B the better deployment.

How to hold a vendor to it

  • Ask for containment and follow-up-contact rate, defined and measured the same way across vendors.
  • Instrument your own follow-up tracking. Do not accept the vendor's resolution definition unexamined.
  • Run a holdout: a slice of traffic the AI never touches, so you have a true baseline to compare against.
